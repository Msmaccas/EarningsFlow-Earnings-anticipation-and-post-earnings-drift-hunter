### Key observations

* **Event‑driven backtesting tools** excel at execution and simulation but treat earnings as simple timestamped events without capturing the nuance of surprise magnitude, guidance updates or crowding risk【379203690481548†L463-L497】.  They rarely provide a preparatory board ranking names by potential interest.
* **Research workspaces** like AlphaSense or Sentieo centralize documents and provide AI‑powered search and summarization【193070278823611†L170-L179】, yet they stop short of converting raw information into prioritized trading setups or drift diagnostics.
* **Multi‑agent trading frameworks** demonstrate that specialized agents can collaborate to make better decisions【167139358718555†L67-L82】【255970564101302†L92-L104】, but existing systems target factor trading or broad market signals rather than the earnings lifecycle.  No competitor combines pre‑event prep, post‑print reaction classification, drift tracking, and a persistent ledger.

These gaps create an opportunity for **EarningsFlow** to build a continuously operating, earnings‑specific observation system that bridges research, analysis and action.  By structuring earnings events into entities (`earnings_event`, `pre_event_context`, `reaction_snapshot`, `post_event_drift_case`, and `thesis_revision`) and using specialized agents for numbers, guidance, transcripts, chart context, options context and scepticism, EarningsFlow aims to provide ranked watchlists, reaction anatomy pages and drift watch boards that existing tools do not offer.
