# Benchmark Ladder

This ladder organizes reference products into tiers—from tools operating in the same earnings‑focused niche to cross‑domain gold standards.  For each tier we summarise what those tools illustrate and extract lessons for **EarningsFlow**.  The goal is not to copy existing implementations but to learn from their strengths and avoid their weaknesses.

### Overarching insights

* **Structure beats unstructured data**: Leading backtesting and research platforms organize information into structured entities before analysis (e.g., events in Blueshift; transcripts and filings in AlphaSense).  EarningsFlow should model earnings events with explicit fields for expectations, results, guidance, price reactions and drift.
* **Multi‑agent collaboration is emerging**: Frameworks like TradingAgents and AlphaCrafter demonstrate that specialized agents can collectively outperform monolithic models【255970564101302†L92-L104】【167139358718555†L67-L82】.  EarningsFlow should leverage this pattern with domain‑specific agents to process numbers, guidance, transcripts, charts and options separately and then combine their assessments.
* **Users value interpretability**: Tools like Symphony’s transcript analysis provide clear sentiment clusters and themes【51208315052190†L162-L190】, while research platforms like AlphaSense deliver executive‑ready reports with citations【193070278823611†L170-L179】.  EarningsFlow must produce human‑readable prep boards and reaction anatomy pages that explain why a name is worth stalking or why a move looks over‑ or under‑reacted.
* **Gaps exist around post‑earnings drift**: Many platforms deliver earnings dates and call summaries but rarely track whether a move follows through or fades.  The FMP article describes post‑earnings drift as a persistent anomaly where prices continue to move in the direction of the earnings surprise【945497615592511†L64-L102】.  EarningsFlow should implement drift tracking and classification to complete the earnings lifecycle.
