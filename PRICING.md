# Pricing & Packaging

EarningsFlow is currently an open‑source research tool designed for demonstration and experimentation.  While no commercial plan exists today, this document outlines how the product could be packaged if brought to market.  These tiers are **hypothetical** and do not constitute an offer.

| Tier | Description | Indicative pricing (per user per month) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Community** | Access to the open‑source code, fixtures and basic anticipation/reaction/drift pipelines.  Users self‑host and manage their own data feeds.  No support or SLAs. | Free (MIT‑licensed) |
| **Professional** | Hosted version with automated data ingestion (earnings calendars, consensus estimates, price and options feeds) and daily scheduled runs.  Includes transcript and options agents with limited quotas, interactive dashboard and API access.  Suitable for small hedge funds and active traders. | \$99–199 |
| **Enterprise** | Everything in Professional plus dedicated support, custom data connectors (internal research repositories, CRM systems), advanced role‑based access control and compliance logging.  May include custom agent tuning and white‑labelling.  Minimum seats and annual commitment required. | Custom (negotiated) |

## Notes

* **No financial advice** – regardless of tier, EarningsFlow only organizes and classifies earnings information.  It does not recommend trades and should not be relied upon as a sole decision‑making source.
* **Data costs** – pricing excludes the cost of licensed market data (e.g. price feeds, options data).  Users may be required to procure data directly from providers.
* **Custom development** – integration with proprietary data sources or bespoke analytics can be arranged under the Enterprise tier at additional cost.
* **Roadmap** – new capabilities (e.g. macro event analysis, voice integration, options context) may change tier structure or pricing.  Feedback from early adopters will guide packaging decisions.
